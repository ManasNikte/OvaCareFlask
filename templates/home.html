What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder common among women of reproductive age. It is characterized by irregular menstrual periods, excess hair growth, acne, and obesity. Women with PCOS may have infrequent or prolonged menstrual cycles, and their ovaries may develop numerous small collections of fluid (follicles) and fail to regularly release eggs. PCOS can lead to long-term health issues such as diabetes, heart disease, and infertility, but it can be managed with lifestyle changes and medical treatments.
